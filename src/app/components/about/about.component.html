ProtMiscuity

ProtMiscuity is a manually curated database of promiscuous proteins that aims to centralize our current knowledge of the promiscuity phenomenon. The collection of protein data was recruited from bibliography and extensively linked with other databases with structural, functional and conformational information. ProtMiscuity provides a useful protein dataset for exploring new possible catalytic activities or substrates, as well as complete information for understanding the relationship between structure and function. It could also help in the development of novel predictive tools ofprotein features.

ProtMiscuity can be browsed by protein name or UniProt ID, or through the information related with its canonical and promiscuous activities as represented by EC numbers. Functional information is linked with structural and conformational information available in PDB and CoDNaS databases. Users can get protein information by searching with their names or Uniprot codes, or looking for the substrates and products related with them. A typical query such as the protein name retrieves general information about its protein family, source organism, the number of promiscuous and canonical reactions in which it is involved and the number of structures related.

Implementation

The database was developed through the implementation of web-scraping and text mining techniques using standard libraries in the programming language Python. More than 500 scientific publications were thus recruited from the different databases and scientific journals websites, which were then inspected manually for their relevance for protein promiscuity. 301 publications were finally selected, based on text criteria, and they were extensively revised to extract the important data for the database. From this process we obtained a total of 65 promiscuous proteins with one or more characterized promiscuous activities, representing 42 different organisms and corresponding to 2480 PDB structures (see Stats)

Reactions, both promiscuous and canonical, were characterized by information available in the literature: substrates, products, Km, Kcat, active site definition and reaction conditions. Likewise, substrates and products related to each reaction described were linked to the information available in PDB Ligand Expo (Sitzmann et al. 2012) and PubChem (Kim et al. 2016) to obtain possible ligands with chemical similarity. This allows the user to explore alternatives for future experimental and industrial applications, biotechnological and drug design.

In order to provide the users with conformational as well as functional information, each protein was linked to other databases such as PDB, CoDNaS, KEGG, Uniprot, Catalytic Site Atlas and QuickGo.The responsive online interface of ProtMiscuity was implemented in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Angular and Node.JS.

Example

The Bovine serum albumin is the main protein of plasma and also a very studied promiscuous protein. It has a good binding capacity for water, Ca(2+), Na(+), K(+), fatty acids, hormones, bilirubin and drugs. Its main function is the regulation of the colloidal osmotic pressure of blood. It is a major zinc transporter in plasma, typically binding about 80% of all plasma zinc. Bovin serum albumin has 8 conformers in CoDNaS with an average RMSD of 0.76 A.

The bovine serum albumin (BSA) has been applied to different promiscuous reactions such as the Henry reaction and several carbon-carbon bond-forming reactions (Ardanaz S. et. Al 2018, Reetz MT. et al 2007, Albanese DCM et al 2015).

In Protmiscuity, the BSA has 4 promiscuous different reactions (Henry reaction, Markonikov addition, Kemp elimination and Biginelli reaction) associated with the same active site (Lys 222), located in its major cavity. Some of the conditions that promotes these promiscuous reactions are the neat organic solvent presence (acetone, ethanol) or mixtures of solvents with water. High temperatures favor the catalysis too. Aniline and dihydroxyacetone are examples of substrates on which BSA can act.
